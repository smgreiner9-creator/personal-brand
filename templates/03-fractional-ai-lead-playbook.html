Fractional AI Lead Playbook

forge studios — retainer delivery guide

Overview

Ongoing monthly engagement. You embed with the client's team as their AI lead. Weekly check-ins, continuous improvements, new automations as needs emerge. This is your highest-value offer — recurring revenue, deep relationships, and compounding impact.

This is the goal. Strategy sessions fill the pipeline. Build sprints prove you deliver. Retainers are where the business stabilizes.

Onboarding Call (60 min)

Run this like a strategy session but deeper. You're not visiting — you're moving in.

Agenda: - (15 min) meet the team. who does what, who are the decision-makers, who are the doers? - (15 min) current AI landscape. what's already in place, what's been tried, what failed? - (15 min) immediate priorities. what are the top 3 things keeping leadership up at night? - (15 min) working rhythm. agree on communication norms: - how do they prefer updates? (slack, email, notion) - when are weekly syncs? (pick a consistent day/time) - what's the escalation path if something's urgent?

Quarterly Business Review (every 3 months)

Purpose

Step back from the week-to-week and evaluate: is this engagement delivering real value? where should we focus next quarter?

QBR Agenda (60 min)

  • (15 min) quarter in review
  • what we set out to do vs. what we actually shipped
  • metrics: where we moved the needle, where we didn't

  • honest assessment of what worked and what didn't

  • (15 min) ROI analysis

  • time saved across the team (hours/week x 13 weeks)
  • revenue impact (if applicable)
  • cost avoidance (what they would have spent on hires, tools, or agencies)

  • compare total impact against the retainer cost

  • (15 min) next quarter roadmap

  • what are the business priorities for the next 90 days?
  • where does AI fit into those priorities?

  • propose 3-5 initiatives, prioritized

  • (15 min) engagement health check

  • is the communication rhythm working?
  • does the team feel supported?
  • anything to adjust about how we work together?

After the QBR

  • send a written summary within 48 hours
  • update the 90-day roadmap
  • this is also the natural moment to discuss scope expansion if the engagement has grown

Day-to-Day Operations

Communication Norms

  • respond to slack/discord messages within 4 hours during business hours
  • for urgent items, client can text or call (set this boundary — define what "urgent" means)
  • async updates > meetings. default to written updates, meet only when needed.

How to Manage Your Time

You're likely running 2-3 retainer clients simultaneously. Protect your calendar:

  • block build time. 2-3 hour focused blocks, no meetings, no slack.
  • batch syncs. try to put all weekly syncs on the same day.
  • document everything. when you solve a problem, write it down. future-you will thank present-you.
  • use AI. practice what you preach. use aiden/claude to draft reports, write docs, and automate your own admin.

When Requests Exceed Scope

Retainers have natural boundaries. If a client starts asking for things that feel like a build sprint:

  • "that's a great initiative. it's bigger than our weekly rhythm can absorb — it needs a dedicated sprint to do it right. want me to scope it as a separate project?"
  • never let the retainer become a dumping ground for unlimited work
  • the monthly report should show 15-25 hours of meaningful work, not 60 hours of busywork

Pricing and Terms

Monthly Rate

  • small team (1-10 people): $2,500/month
  • mid-size team (10-50 people): $3,500-5,000/month
  • adjust based on complexity, not just team size

Contract Terms

  • month-to-month after initial 3-month commitment
  • 30-day written notice to cancel from either side
  • payment due on the 1st of each month, net 15
  • rate review at each quarterly business review

When to Raise Your Rate

  • after the first QBR shows clear ROI
  • when scope has naturally expanded beyond original agreement
  • when you're at capacity and need to prioritize higher-value clients
  • never raise mid-month. always discuss at QBR and implement next month.

Retention and Growth

Keeping Clients Long-Term

  • ship something every week. even small improvements show momentum.
  • the monthly report is non-negotiable. this is the single biggest retention lever.
  • anticipate needs. don't wait for them to ask. "i noticed your team is spending a lot of time on X — i have an idea for how to automate that."
  • build relationships beyond the decision-maker. when the team loves working with you, the contract stays even if leadership changes.

Warning Signs a Client Might Churn

  • they start canceling weekly syncs
  • response times to your messages slow down
  • they stop sharing context or new priorities with you
  • "let's revisit next month" when you propose initiatives

When you see these signs: have a direct conversation. "i want to make sure this engagement is delivering value. is there anything we should adjust?" sometimes the fix is simple. sometimes the engagement has run its course — and that's okay.

Upselling

  • additional retainer seats: "your marketing team could use the same AI support. want me to extend to them?"
  • build sprints within the retainer: larger initiatives scoped and priced separately
  • referrals: "if you know other founders who could use this, I'd love an intro." always ask. satisfied clients are your best sales channel.
