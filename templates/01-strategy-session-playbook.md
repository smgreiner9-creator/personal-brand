# Strategy Session Playbook
## forge studios — AI strategy session delivery guide

---

## Overview

A 90-minute deep dive into a client's workflow, team, and goals. They walk away with a written roadmap of where AI fits and what to build first. This is your foot-in-the-door offer — low commitment, high value, designed to convert into a build sprint or retainer.

---

## Before the Call (1-2 days prior)

### Research the Client
- [ ] Visit their website, LinkedIn, and any product demos
- [ ] Understand their industry and competitors
- [ ] Note their team size (ask if unknown)
- [ ] Check their current tech stack (BuiltWith, Wappalyzer, or just ask)
- [ ] Find any public pain points (reviews, social posts, job listings that hint at gaps)

### Send Pre-Session Questionnaire
Email this to the client at least 24 hours before the session.

> Subject: prep for our strategy session on [date]
>
> hey [name],
>
> looking forward to our session. to make the most of our 90 minutes, i'd love some context upfront. no need to write essays — bullet points are great.
>
> 1. what does your team do day-to-day? walk me through a typical week.
> 2. where do you feel the most friction or wasted time?
> 3. have you tried any AI tools already? what worked, what didn't?
> 4. if AI could solve one problem for you tomorrow, what would it be?
> 5. what does your tech stack look like? (tools, platforms, databases)
> 6. team size and who would be involved in implementing changes?
> 7. any budget constraints i should know about?
>
> talk soon,
> [your name]

## Session Agenda (90 minutes)

### Block 1: Discovery (25 min)
**Goal:** understand their world before prescribing anything.

- (5 min) introductions, set expectations for the session
- (10 min) walk me through your workflow end-to-end
  - where does work come in?
  - what are the handoff points?
  - where do things slow down or break?
- (10 min) dig into pain points from questionnaire
  - "you mentioned [X] — tell me more about that"
  - ask "why" at least twice to get past surface-level answers

**Key questions to ask:**
- "what does this process look like when it goes wrong?"
- "how many hours per week does your team spend on [repetitive task]?"
- "if you could clone one person on your team, who would it be and why?"

### Block 2: AI Opportunity Mapping (25 min)
**Goal:** identify where AI creates the most leverage.

- (10 min) categorize their pain points into three buckets:
  1. **automate** — repetitive tasks AI can handle end-to-end
  2. **augment** — tasks where AI assists a human (drafts, research, triage)
  3. **not yet** — too complex, too risky, or not enough ROI
- (10 min) for each "automate" and "augment" item, explain:
  - what the AI solution looks like in plain english
  - rough effort level (days vs weeks vs months)
  - expected impact (hours saved, error reduction, speed improvement)
- (5 min) identify quick wins vs. strategic bets
  - quick wins: can ship in under a week, immediate time savings
  - strategic bets: bigger lift, bigger payoff over 3-6 months

### Block 3: Roadmap and Prioritization (25 min)
**Goal:** turn the mapping into a sequenced action plan.

- (10 min) build the priority matrix together:
  - x-axis: effort (low to high)
  - y-axis: impact (low to high)
  - top-left quadrant = start here
- (10 min) sequence the top 3-5 initiatives into a 30-day plan:
  - week 1-2: quick win #1
  - week 2-3: quick win #2
  - week 3-4: begin strategic initiative
- (5 min) discuss what's needed to execute:
  - do they have the team internally?
  - do they need external help? (this is where you plant the seed for a build sprint)

### Block 4: Wrap-up and Next Steps (15 min)
**Goal:** clear next steps, open the door to further engagement.

- (5 min) recap the top 3 takeaways
- (5 min) answer any remaining questions
- (5 min) discuss next steps:
  - "i'll have the written roadmap to you within 48 hours"
  - "if you want help executing on [top priority], that's exactly what a build sprint covers — happy to scope that out if you're interested"
  - do NOT hard sell. let the value of the session do the work.

## After the Call (within 48 hours)

### Deliver the Roadmap Document
Send a clean PDF or notion doc with:

1. **Executive Summary** (3-4 sentences)
   - who they are, what we found, what to do first

2. **Current State Assessment**
   - workflow overview (as you understood it)
   - key pain points identified
   - current tool/tech landscape

3. **AI Opportunity Map**
   - table format: opportunity | type (automate/augment) | effort | impact | priority
   - include the ones you recommended against, with reasoning (builds trust)

4. **30-Day Action Plan**
   - week-by-week breakdown
   - specific deliverables for each week
   - who owns what (their team vs. external help)

5. **Recommended Next Steps**
   - what they can do internally
   - where external expertise would accelerate things
   - optional: "if you'd like forge studios to lead the build, here's what a sprint would look like"

### Follow-Up Sequence
- **Day 2:** send the roadmap with a short note
- **Day 7:** check in — "any questions after reviewing the roadmap?"
- **Day 14:** if no response, one more touch — "wanted to see if you've started on any of the quick wins. happy to help if you hit any walls."
- **After that:** add to newsletter, engage on social, let the relationship breathe

## Pricing Notes (internal only)

- target range: $500-750
- price based on company size: solopreneurs/small teams at $500, established SMBs at $750
- always frame as investment vs. cost: "the roadmap alone will save you months of trial and error"
- if they push back on price, do not discount. instead, emphasize the deliverable: "you're getting a written roadmap your team can execute on for months"

---

## Conversion Targets

- 30-40% of strategy sessions should convert to a build sprint or retainer
- if conversion is below 20%, the session isn't delivering enough value — revisit the roadmap quality
- if conversion is above 50%, you might be underpricing the session
